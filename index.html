About Me

Born on a beautiful island, Sri Lanka, I have spent recent years hopping between islands, and my path through computer science has unfolded in much the same way: moving from shore to shore, crossing boundaries, and discovering new worlds along the way. Each stop has revealed a different dimension of the discipline, from distributed systems to cryptography, all bound by a fascination with how independent actors learn to coexist, coordinate, and trust.

Code becomes more than logic in decentralized systems where it becomes a social contract. The most elegant protocols are not those that merely function, but those that encourage fairness, align incentives, and cultivate resilience. This is the space where my work lives: at the intersection of cryptography and cooperation, shaping the quiet rules that allow trust to emerge where no central authority stands.

My current harbor is Ireland, where I earned my PhD and now navigate the frontiers of research as a Postdoctoral Researcher at University College Dublin, contributing to the National Space Subsystems & Payloads Initiative. My work explores how blockchain technologies can be interlaced with satellite communication systems, enhancing their security, scalability, and fairness as these networks stretch beyond the bounds of traditional, centralized control.
